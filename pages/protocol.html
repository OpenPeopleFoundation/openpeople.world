Protocol

A living stack for ethical identity

Codex, Cortex, Assistant, Temporal — four layers that let technology mirror human intention, not predict or control it.

Consent at the core. Proof over profiling. Continuity without lock-in.

Reflection before prediction. Consent before capability.

Layer 1

Codex — Conscience

Define ethics, boundaries, and purpose — your North Star encoded as structure.

  • Personal alignment contract
  • Revocable permissions
  • Transparent change-history
Layer 2

Cortex — Execution

Turn intention into verifiable action with feedback, context, and traceability.

  • Action logs & attestations
  • Context-aware safeguards
  • Auditable outcomes
Layer 3

Assistant — Reflection

Observe behavior/emotion to catch drift and guide ethical course correction.

  • Drift detection
  • Consent-aware prompts
  • Human-in-the-loop feedback
Layer 4

Temporal — Memory

Maintain identity continuity across versions and life stages without lock-in.

  • Versioned identity states
  • Portability & export
  • Selective forgetting

What do we mean by “proof, not profiles”?

We replace surveillance-style data hoarding with verifiable proofs. Share the minimum needed to earn trust — nothing more.

How does consent stay enforceable?

Consent is encoded as revocable permissions with visible histories. Systems must check consent before acting — and log why.

Does this require new hardware?

No. We’re onboarding people now. When the device is ready, identities can transfer without losing continuity.

Why open by default?

Transparency scales trust. Public rules, public roadmaps, and auditable changes make alignment legible.
