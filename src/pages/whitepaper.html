Executive Summary

Open People is an open, non-profit protocol for human-aligned digital identity. It provides a framework where technology can represent human intention without replacing it — ensuring identity remains owned, portable, and revocable by the individual.

The protocol combines three lenses: the philosophy of alignment and structural empathy; a technical stack that mirrors human cognition; and operational practices that test alignment in live environments. Together they form a living system that evolves with feedback, context, and consent.

Alignment is not a one-time certification. It is a continuous loop: intention → action → reflection → memory. Open People encodes this loop so it can be observed, measured, and improved without handing control to opaque systems.

I. Philosophical Foundations

Technology should mirror conscience, not exploit attention. The protocol is grounded in three beliefs: structure before power, humanity before machine, and alignment before acceleration. These principles keep systems accountable to the people who rely on them.

Structural empathy is the core idea: intelligence that understands context, honours consent, and remains open to reflection. Instead of optimising for prediction, Open People optimises for meaning — producing verifiable proofs of care, stewardship, and mutual understanding.

Technology becomes ethical when it remembers why we built it.

II. Technical Framework

The Open People protocol is a four-layer stack modelled after human consciousness. Each layer performs a distinct cognitive and ethical function while remaining auditable.

Codex — The Conscience Layer

Encodes human intention as verifiable structure: alignment contracts, consent schemas, and transparency rules.

Cortex — The Execution Layer

Plans and executes actions that respect Codex constraints, generating attestations and feedback loops.

Assistant — The Reflection Layer

Observes behaviour, detects drift, and guides ethical adjustments with human-in-the-loop explanations.

Temporal — The Memory Layer

Anchors history with selective memory, enabling portability, audits, and proofs without surveillance.

The layers are separable yet interoperable. Any organisation can adopt the stack incrementally while keeping data ownership and governance aligned with local norms.

III. Measurement & Metrics

Alignment must be observable. We rely on verifiable signals rather than promises:

  • VR — Variance Reduction: how closely outcomes match declared ethics.
  • DE — Decision Efficiency: safety-checked speed of execution.
  • QAA — Quality-Adjusted Acceptance: trusted outcomes per cycle.
  • FL — Feedback Latency: time from misalignment to detection and correction.
  • CRI — Cultural Resolution Index: fit to local norms and human context.

These signals roll up into a Composite Alignment Score, giving operators early warnings when practice drifts from principle. Metrics are transparent, non-proprietary, and designed for communal verification.

IV. Governance & Open Participation

Governance is ethics made operational. The Foundation stewards the protocol as a non-profit, but power remains distributed through open proposals, rotating ethical review, and community assemblies. Every major decision includes a public rationale, vote record, and feedback window.

Funding is non-control seeking. Research, code, and documentation are open by default. Forkability and portability ensure the community can evolve the protocol if stewardship fails.

This protocol grows through participation, not persuasion.

V. Conclusion & Future Work

Open People shows that ethics can be expressed in code without being reduced to it. When identity remains owned by people, data becomes meaning instead of fuel for extraction.

Next steps focus on live pilots, public alignment ledgers, device continuity, and cultural councils that keep the protocol grounded in human experience. The work is open invitation: join, critique, and build with us.

The future of intelligence is not control; it is coordination.
